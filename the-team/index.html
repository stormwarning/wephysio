The Team

Who We Are

Craig Brososky

Craig Brososky, owner of WePhysio and a clinical educator at the University of Alberta, received both his science degrees from the University of Alberta in Edmonton where he was born and raised. The river valley of Edmonton, which has the largest greenway of any city in North America, has provided him ample opportunity for cycling, running, cross-country skiing, and exploration and has served as a backdrop to both the learning and teaching process that Craig has enjoyed for over twenty years. Along with being a clinical educator and published author of articles on low back pain management, sport ergogenics, and injury prevention, Craig is a registered Injury Management Consultant with the Insurance Bureau of Canada and the College of Physical Therapists. Craig believes you can never stop learning and loves the lifelong challenge of educating both individuals and groups.

Graham Ciciarelli

Graham Ciciarelli completed his Bachelor of Science in occupational therapy at the University of Alberta. His clinical experience during the program included time at the Hinton General Hospital, the Alberta Hospital, Millard Health and in the Hazeldean Early Education Program. After completing his education, he managed a private clinic before working as the regional manager of occupational therapy for CBI Health. In 2006 he founded his own consulting service where he works with employers in the prevention, treatment, and management of injuries related to and sustained outside of work. He has interest and experience in areas that vary from office and industrial ergonomics to return to work planning to functional capacity evaluations.

Graham subscribes to the WePhysio philosophy on wellness and enjoys an active lifestyle himself through cross country skiing, snowshoeing, and mountain biking. Belonging is as important an ingredient in wellness as activity and Graham finds that in his family. He once scored a hole in one golfing with his father on Father’s Day, shares a birthday with his young son, and finds ways to enjoy the outdoors with his family as much as possible.

Dan Bradley

Dan attended the University of British Columbia with dual scholarships — Louise McKinney and UBC President’s Entrance — both for high GPA and after obtaining his undergraduate degree in Human Kinetics moved home to Edmonton where he completed a Masters of Science in Physiotherapy at the University of Alberta. Dan fits into the WePhysio philosophy on wellness by being a supporter of actively pursuing health. He is an avid cyclist, having formerly been a competitive road/track cyclist, has taken courses in the prevention of running injuries and muscle mobilization, and has started post graduate training in manual therapy. In addition to his cycling expertise, Dan has experience working with clients who have suffered strokes, spinal cord injuries, and cardiac surgery. He brings a wealth of knowledge and a thirst for continual learning that will benefit patients.

Mel Muise

Mel Muise embodies the WePhysio definition of performance. She has been a competitive runner for seventeen years and with each run learns something new about herself and how she runs. Every success she realizes and, equally as important, every failure has strengthened her knowledge and enhanced her ability to perform. She is grateful to have been coached by wonderful teachers in the art of running so it seems natural that she is now working on gaining her own coaching certification. Her philosophy is simple: A good running program is based on proven training principles for the development of the complete runner and everyone can master the skills. Mel is continually training for performance events and for each she sets herself challenging performance goals.

Michael Carlos

Michael Carlos was born and raised in Winnipeg, Manitoba where he earned both an arts and science degree in psychology from the University of Winnipeg. He went on to earn his physiotherapy degree from the University of Manitoba in 2009. He spent his physiotherapy internship at the Pan Am Clinic, Winnipeg’s foremost sports medicine clinic. Michael believes that a good physiotherapist is one that can empower you through education and guided exercise.

Outside the clinic Michael prefers an active lifestyle and enjoys running, yoga, bodybuilding, and cycling. He has given numerous injury prevention talks through the Running Room and has completed two full marathons and many half marathons. He finds himself frequently asked by family and friends to design exercise programs to help them reach specific goals, anything from completing a first ever half marathon to obtaining better strength at the gym. He believes in the WePhysio definition of wellness and looks forward to assisting clients in realizing their health and recovery goals.
